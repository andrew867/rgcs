# RGCS Humanity Commons Charter

**Status:** values and stewardship statement. Not a licence, not a
contract, not legal advice. The operative legal terms are in
`LICENSE` and `LICENSE_MAP.md`.

## Preamble

RGCS began as an attempt to find out whether unusual claims about
resonance, coordinates, memory and spacetime could be translated into
mathematics and then attacked by software and evidence.

> Lore proposes. Mathematics translates. Software attacks.
> Evidence decides. Provenance remembers.

Most of what the programme produced is negative. That is the point,
and the negative results are published alongside everything else.

## Article 1 — the foundation is common infrastructure

These are treated as common technical knowledge and should remain
available to everyone:

- coordinate and root specifications;
- frame, epoch and time-scale declarations;
- uncertainty and covariance methods;
- provenance schemas;
- evidence classifications;
- negative-result registers;
- interoperability protocols and test vectors;
- reference tests;
- public mathematical derivations.

**No steward may claim ownership over basic mathematics, physical
law, or spacetime.** A coordinate is a description. Naming a place
does not create a right to it.

## Article 2 — claims stay typed

Every public result carries its evidence class. The ladder is not
decoration; it is enforced in code, and software may not emit a
physical class.

```
SOURCE_CLAIM · DERIVED_ARITHMETIC · ANALYTIC_MODEL
NUMERICAL_SIMULATION · SYNTHETIC_RUN
BENCH_MEASUREMENT · INDEPENDENT_REPLICATION
UNSUPPORTED · NOT_APPLICABLE · REFUSED
```

Removing, weakening or reassigning an evidence label to make a result
look stronger is the one act this charter treats as a breach of good
faith.

## Article 3 — negative results are first-class

The registers of failures, refusals, corrections and prior-art
defeats ship with the release and must travel with any redistribution
that presents itself as RGCS. A fork that keeps the machinery and
drops the negative results is not this project.

## Article 4 — commerce is welcome, enclosure is not

Permitted and encouraged:

- commercial products, services and support;
- hardware, calibration and integration businesses;
- consulting, training and certification;
- funded maintenance and assurance work.

Opposed:

- private enclosure of the open specification;
- patent claims over the basic mathematics or coordinate
  infrastructure published here;
- redistribution that strips provenance, attribution, evidence
  labels or safety boundaries.

## Article 5 — provenance and attribution

Source claims keep their original wording, their origin, and their
status. Corrections stay visible next to what they corrected rather
than replacing it. History is preserved, including the parts that
were wrong.

## Article 6 — safety boundaries are not optional

The refusals in `SCIENTIFIC_BOUNDARIES.md` and `NON_CLAIMS.md` are
part of the work. No medical claims, no human or animal testing, no
uncontrolled hazardous apparatus, and no presentation of an unproven
physical layer as established.

## Article 7 — stewardship

Long-term stewardship may transfer to a purpose-bound non-profit,
foundation, cooperative or trust whose charter preserves Articles 1
through 6. Until then the author is the steward, and this document is
the standard the author asks to be held to.

## Article 8 — what this charter does not do

It does not grant, extend, restrict or modify any licence. It does
not create a patent covenant — see `PATENT_NON_ASSERTION_INTENT.md`,
which is itself marked as intent pending legal review. It cannot
bind third parties, and it makes no promise about the physical
hypotheses the programme has been unable to test.

## The question

The programme is pursued because the question seemed worth asking,
not because any particular answer was assumed:

> It appears possible. Can it be proved, implemented, measured, or
> honestly refused?

So far the honest answer, for the physical layer, has been *refused*
or *unmeasured* every time. That is recorded rather than hidden.
