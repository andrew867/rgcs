# T06b — Observer effects and temporal permanence

Coverage: **C028, C005, C004**. Gate: **G36**. Lane: **quarantined**.

## The distinction that does all the work

**Observer-frame** ≠ **observer-causes-reality**.

That one line separates a legitimate reformulation from a category
error, and most of the confusion in this area is a slide between them.

| Reading | Status | Comment |
|---|---|---|
| Observation is always from a frame; descriptions are perspectival | uncontroversial | true, and says nothing about causation |
| Measurement in QM involves an interaction that changes the system | established physics | about *interaction*, not about minds |
| A conscious observer collapses the wavefunction and stabilizes matter | `SOURCE_HYPOTHESIS` | no evidence; not what QM says |

Decoherence accounts for measurement-like behaviour with no conscious
observer anywhere in the formalism. A photomultiplier decoheres a
superposition whether or not anyone reads the output.

## C028 — permanence through other observers (`SOURCE_HYPOTHESIS`)

The claim: things persist because other observers hold them.

The ordinary account: things persist because the **environment** is
persistent and continuously interacting. A rock in an empty room stays
a rock. Object permanence in cognition is a *model* of that
persistence, and the model existing does not make the persistence
depend on the model.

**Not falsifiable as stated** — retained as metaphor, which is the
honest status rather than a dismissal.

## What is real here

Memory and record continuity, social cross-validation, and shared
environments genuinely do stabilize **our account** of the world.
Multiple observers correct each other's errors. That is epistemology
working correctly, and it is the strongest true version of the
intuition C028 is reaching for.

## Failure modes tested against

Sensory leakage, suggestion, memory reconstruction (memory is
reconstructive, not a recording — this alone explains a great deal of
what feels like observer-dependent reality), social consensus effects,
and instrument artifacts.

## Boundary

**Do not claim observers stabilize physical matter.** No experiment in
this programme addresses it, and no result here supports it.
