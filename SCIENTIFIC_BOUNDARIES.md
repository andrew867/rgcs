What this project has **not** established. Every line is enforced by
tests, refusal functions, or both.

## Specific refusals, with reasons

**Metric actuation — refused by arithmetic, not by policy.** Every
apparatus configuration, including a deliberately absurd 2 MJ bound
far beyond anything buildable, sits at least 17.8 decades below the
most sensitive instrument ever built. Raising a clock by one
millimetre produces a fractional shift 6.6 × 10¹⁷ times larger than
the entire apparatus. This does not depend on any source claim being
right or wrong.

**Sovereign navigation — unsupported.** A local clock-rate Jacobian
is rank 1 against three position unknowns and is constant over an
equipotential surface. Separately, zero of seven signal-denied
methods are infrastructure-free; each substitutes a catalogue, map,
model, second clock or initial condition for the signal it avoids.

**Phryll — no detection state exists.** There is no `PHRYLL_DETECTED`
value anywhere in the codebase, and a test greps for it. The ladder
terminates at `CANDIDATE_NEW_MECHANISM`.

**Passive self-oscillation — refused.** A passive resonator has no
gain element and can only ring down.

**Quartz four-state spin memory — BLOCKED**, all qualification gates
open.

**Planetary polyhedral symmetry — no result possible.** The
repository ships no geophysical data; the audit reports
`NO_REAL_DATA`.

**Biological and medical claims — not tested, by policy.** Nine
claims are registered `REFUSED_NOT_TESTED`. No human or animal
exposure, treatment, diagnosis or therapeutic recommendation is
permitted. Any future biological lane requires an independent
medical and ethics programme with institutional oversight.

## Safety

No open 2.45 GHz radiator. No uncontrolled laser, RF, high voltage,
vacuum or pressure. Mains construction requires a qualified person.
Optical work must be enclosed with no exposed beam at eye height.

## What the programme has established

Mathematics and software, tested and prior-art mapped. Several
corrections to its own earlier claims. A large register of negative
results. An integration whose value still requires external
evaluation.

That is a smaller claim than the project's origins suggested, and it
is the accurate one.
